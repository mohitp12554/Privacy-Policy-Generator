Personal information is often anything that may be accustomed identify a person, not limited to the person's name, address, date of birth, legal status, contact information, ID issue, and expiry date, financial records, credit information, anamnesis, where one travels, and intentions to accumulate goods and services.

Online Privacy Policy Certification Programs

Online certification or "seal" programs are an example of industry self-regulation of privacy policies. Seal programs usually require the implementation of fair information practices as determined by the certification program and should require continued compliance monitoring.

TRUSTArc (formerly TRUSTe), the primary online privacy seal program, included over 1,800 members by 2007 Other online seal programs include the Trust Guard Privacy Verified program, eTrust, and Web trust.

Some websites also define their privacy policies using P3P or Internet Content Rating Association (ICRA), allowing browsers to automatically assess the amount of privacy offered by the site and allowing access only if the site's privacy practices are in line with the user's privacy settings.
